SECTION 1: COMPANY OVERVIEW

SECTION 2: EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

2.1 Equal Employment Opportunity
ACME Corporation is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other legally protected characteristic.

2.2 At-Will Employment
Employment at ACME is at-will, meaning either the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice, subject to applicable law.

2.3 Background Checks
All offers of employment are contingent upon successful completion of a background check. This may include verification of employment history, education, criminal records, and credit history where permitted by law.

2.4 Onboarding
New employees undergo a structured 30-day onboarding program that includes:
- Day 1-5: Orientation, IT setup, HR paperwork, company culture introduction
- Day 6-15: Department-specific training, meeting key stakeholders
- Day 16-30: Role-specific projects with mentorship support
- 30-Day Check-in: Formal review with manager to assess progress

SECTION 3: CODE OF CONDUCT

3.1 Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally. This includes:
- Treating colleagues, clients, and partners with respect
- Maintaining confidentiality of proprietary information
- Avoiding conflicts of interest
- Following all company policies and procedures

3.2 Anti-Harassment Policy
ACME has zero tolerance for harassment of any kind. Harassment includes unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristics that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment. All complaints will be investigated promptly and thoroughly.

3.3 Conflict of Interest
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests. Any potential conflicts must be disclosed to HR immediately. This includes:
- Outside employment that competes with ACME
- Financial interests in competitors or suppliers
- Personal relationships that could influence business decisions

3.4 Confidentiality
Employees must protect confidential company information, including trade secrets, financial data, customer information, and strategic plans. This obligation continues after employment ends.

SECTION 4: REMOTE WORK GUIDELINES

4.1 Eligibility
All full-time employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period are eligible for remote work arrangements, subject to manager approval and role requirements.

4.2 Remote Work Schedule
Employees may work remotely up to three (3) days per week with manager approval. Remote schedules must ensure adequate team coverage and be submitted through HR Connect by the 1st of each month.

4.3 Core Hours
Remote workers must be available during core hours: 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM in their local time zone. Outside core hours, flexible scheduling is permitted with manager agreement.

4.4 Equipment & Workspace
- ACME provides a laptop, monitor, and keyboard for remote work
- Employees must maintain a dedicated, secure workspace
- Internet speed must be at least 50 Mbps download / 10 Mbps upload
- ACME provides a $500 annual home office stipend

4.5 Security Requirements
- Company-approved VPN must be used for all work activities
- Work must not be performed on public Wi-Fi without VPN
- Screens must be locked when stepping away
- Company data must not be stored on personal devices

SECTION 5: COMPENSATION & BENEFITS

5.1 Pay Structure
ACME uses a grade-based pay structure with salary bands for each role level:
- Level 1 (Entry): $55,000 - $75,000
- Level 2 (Mid): $75,000 - $105,000
- Level 3 (Senior): $105,000 - $145,000
- Level 4 (Lead/Staff): $140,000 - $190,000
- Level 5 (Principal/Director): $180,000 - $260,000

5.2 Pay Schedule
Employees are paid bi-weekly on alternating Fridays via direct deposit.

5.3 Health Insurance
ACME offers three health plan options:
- Basic Plan: $0 employee premium, $2,000 deductible
- Standard Plan: $75/month employee premium, $1,000 deductible
- Premium Plan: $150/month employee premium, $500 deductible
All plans include dental and vision coverage. Coverage begins on the first day of the month following hire date.

5.4 Retirement Benefits
- 401(k) plan with company match up to 4% of salary
- Vesting schedule: 25% per year, fully vested after 4 years
- Employees can contribute up to IRS annual limits

5.5 Additional Benefits
- Life insurance: 2x annual salary (company-paid)
- Short-term disability: 60% of salary for up to 12 weeks
- Long-term disability: 50% of salary
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP): Free counseling and support services
- Professional development budget: $2,000 per year
- Gym membership reimbursement: Up to $75/month

SECTION 6: PAID TIME OFF

6.1 Vacation Days
- Years 0-2: 15 days per year (accrued at 1.25 days/month)
- Years 3-5: 20 days per year (accrued at 1.67 days/month)
- Years 6+: 25 days per year (accrued at 2.08 days/month)

6.2 Carryover Policy
Unused vacation days may be carried over up to a maximum of 5 days into the following calendar year. Days exceeding the carryover limit will be forfeited on January 1st.

6.3 Sick Leave
Employees receive 10 sick days per year. Sick leave does not carry over and resets on January 1st. A doctor's note is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

6.4 Personal Days
Employees receive 3 personal days per year for personal matters. These do not carry over.

6.5 Holidays
ACME observes the following paid holidays:
- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- Presidents' Day
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving Day
- Day after Thanksgiving
- Christmas Eve
- Christmas Day
- New Year's Eve (half day)

6.6 Parental Leave
- Primary caregiver: 16 weeks paid leave
- Secondary caregiver: 6 weeks paid leave
- Adoption/Foster: 12 weeks paid leave

SECTION 7: PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

7.1 Review Cycle
Performance reviews are conducted semi-annually:
- Mid-Year Review: June (informal check-in, goal adjustment)
- Annual Review: December (formal evaluation, compensation review)

7.2 Rating Scale
- Exceptional (5): Consistently exceeds expectations in all areas
- Exceeds Expectations (4): Frequently exceeds expectations
- Meets Expectations (3): Consistently meets role requirements
- Needs Improvement (2): Falls short in some areas
- Unsatisfactory (1): Fails to meet minimum requirements

7.3 Goal Setting
Employees set 3-5 SMART goals at the beginning of each year in collaboration with their manager. Goals are reviewed and adjusted at the mid-year review.

SECTION 8: DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

8.1 Progressive Discipline
ACME follows a progressive discipline approach:
1. Verbal Warning: Documented conversation with manager
2. Written Warning: Formal written notice with improvement plan
3. Final Written Warning: Last chance with specific timeline
4. Termination: If issues are not resolved

8.2 Immediate Termination
Certain behaviors may result in immediate termination without progressive discipline:
- Theft or fraud
- Violence or threats of violence
- Harassment or discrimination
- Violation of confidentiality agreements
- Being under the influence of drugs/alcohol at work
- Gross insubordination

SECTION 9: HEALTH & SAFETY

9.1 Workplace Safety
ACME is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment. All employees must:
- Report unsafe conditions immediately
- Follow all safety protocols and procedures
- Participate in required safety training
- Use personal protective equipment when required

9.2 Emergency Procedures
Each office has posted emergency procedures including:
- Fire evacuation routes and assembly points
- Severe weather sheltering locations
- First aid kit locations
- AED (defibrillator) locations

9.3 Ergonomics
ACME provides ergonomic assessments for all workstations. Employees can request an assessment through IT Services. Standing desk converters are available upon request.

---

SECTION 10: SEPARATION OF EMPLOYMENT

10.1 Resignation
Employees are requested to provide at least two (2) weeks written notice of resignation. Managers and above are requested to provide four (4) weeks notice.

10.2 Exit Process
Upon separation, employees must:
- Return all company property (laptop, badge, keys, etc.)
- Complete knowledge transfer documentation
- Attend an exit interview with HR
- Sign acknowledgment of continuing obligations (confidentiality, non-compete where applicable)

10.3 Final Pay
Final paychecks, including accrued unused vacation, will be processed on the next regular pay date following separation.
