ACME CORPORATION - EXPENSE AND TRAVEL POLICY
===============================================
Finance Department
Version 4.0 | Effective: January 1, 2025

---

SECTION 1: PURPOSE

This policy establishes guidelines for business-related expenses and travel for all ACME Corporation employees. It ensures expenses are reasonable, properly documented, and compliant with tax regulations.

---

SECTION 2: EXPENSE GUIDELINES

2.1 General Principles
- All expenses must have a legitimate business purpose
- Expenses should be reasonable and cost-effective
- Original receipts are required for all expenses over $25
- Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of incurring the expense
- Late submissions (>60 days) will not be reimbursed without VP approval

2.2 Approval Thresholds
- Up to $500: Manager approval
- $501 - $2,500: Director approval
- $2,501 - $10,000: VP approval
- Over $10,000: CFO approval

2.3 Corporate Credit Card
- Issued to employees at Level 3 (Senior) and above
- Monthly credit limit: $5,000 (higher limits by request)
- Personal charges on corporate cards are strictly prohibited
- Card must be reconciled monthly in the expense system

---

SECTION 3: TRAVEL POLICY

3.1 Booking Procedures
- All travel must be booked through the company travel portal (Navan/TripActions)
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible (lower fares)
- Same-day or next-day travel requires Director approval
- International travel requires VP approval

3.2 Air Travel
Domestic Flights:
- Economy class for flights under 4 hours
- Premium economy for flights over 4 hours
- Book lowest logical fare (within 2 hours of preferred time)

International Flights:
- Economy class for flights under 6 hours
- Premium economy for flights 6-10 hours
- Business class for flights over 10 hours (VP and above: all international flights)

General Rules:
- Book refundable fares only when itinerary is uncertain
- Frequent flyer miles earned on business travel belong to the employee
- Seat upgrades using personal miles/points are permitted
- Baggage fees for first checked bag are reimbursable

3.3 Hotels
- Use preferred hotel partners when available (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt)
- Nightly rate limits (excluding taxes):
  - Tier 1 cities (SF, NYC, LA, Chicago, Boston, DC): $275/night
  - Tier 2 cities (all other US cities): $200/night
  - International: Varies by location (see travel portal for limits)
- Room service is limited to $30/day when no restaurant is reasonably available
- Minibar charges are not reimbursable
- Laundry is reimbursable for trips exceeding 4 nights

3.4 Ground Transportation
- Ride-sharing (Uber/Lyft) preferred over taxis when available
- UberX/Lyft standard class for individual travel
- UberXL/Lyft XL when traveling with 3+ colleagues
- Rental cars: Compact or mid-size; full-size for 3+ travelers
  - Always purchase collision damage waiver (CDW)
  - Refuel before returning (fuel surcharges are not reimbursable)
- Personal vehicle mileage: Reimbursed at IRS standard rate ($0.67/mile for 2025)
- Parking and tolls are reimbursable with receipts

3.5 Meals During Travel
Per diem meal allowances (including tip):
- Breakfast: $20
- Lunch: $30
- Dinner: $50
- Total daily cap: $100

Alternatively, employees may submit actual receipts up to the daily cap. Alcohol is limited to $25 per day during business travel.

---

SECTION 4: CLIENT ENTERTAINMENT

4.1 Client Meals
- Reasonable restaurant expenses for client meetings
- Per-person limit: $100 for dinner, $50 for lunch, $30 for breakfast
- Must document: client name(s), company, business purpose
- Alcohol is permitted but should be moderate

4.2 Client Events
- Sporting events, concerts, etc.: Up to $250/person with Director approval
- Must have clear business purpose documented
- ACME employee must attend the event with the client

4.3 Gifts
- Business gifts to clients/partners: Up to $100 per person per year
- Must comply with the recipient's company gift policy
- No cash or cash-equivalent gifts
- Document recipient name, company, and business purpose

---

SECTION 5: WORK-FROM-HOME EXPENSES

5.1 Home Office Stipend
- $500 annual stipend for home office setup (furniture, equipment)
- Paid as a lump sum in January each year
- New hires receive prorated amount based on start date

5.2 Internet Reimbursement
- Up to $75/month for home internet (remote workers only)
- Must work remotely at least 3 days/week to qualify
- Submit monthly internet bill for reimbursement

5.3 Office Supplies
- Reasonable office supplies (pens, notebooks, printer paper) are reimbursable
- Orders over $50 should go through the office supply portal

---

SECTION 6: PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT

6.1 Training and Courses
- Up to $2,000/year for professional development (company standard)
- Engineering department: Up to $3,000/year (department-specific allocation)
- Online courses, certifications, workshops, and seminars qualify
- Must be relevant to current role or career growth at ACME

6.2 Conference Attendance
- Registration fees: Reimbursable with manager approval
- Travel expenses: Follow standard travel policy
- Maximum 2 conferences per year per employee
- Post-conference: Share learnings with team (presentation or summary)

6.3 Books and Subscriptions
- Technical books and professional subscriptions: Reimbursable up to $500/year
- Request through the learning portal or submit receipts

---

SECTION 7: NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES

The following expenses are NOT reimbursable:
- Personal entertainment
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, or moving violations
- Personal phone bills (unless pre-approved BYOD stipend)
- Pet care or pet travel
- Personal grooming (haircuts, clothing unless company-branded)
- Gym memberships (covered separately under benefits)
- First-class air travel (unless pre-approved for medical reasons)
- Spouse/partner travel expenses
- Lost or stolen personal property
- Minibar charges
- In-room movies
- Childcare during travel (except for emergency situations with VP approval)

---

SECTION 8: EXPENSE REPORTING

8.1 How to Submit
1. Log into the expense management system (Expensify)
2. Create a new report or add to existing monthly report
3. Upload receipt photos (mobile app recommended)
4. Categorize each expense appropriately
5. Add business purpose description
6. Submit for approval

8.2 Required Documentation
Each expense must include:
- Date of expense
- Amount and currency
- Vendor/merchant name
- Business purpose (1-2 sentences)
- Receipt (photo or scan for expenses over $25)
- Client name (if applicable)

8.3 Reimbursement Timeline
- Approved domestic expenses: Reimbursed within 5 business days
- Approved international expenses: Reimbursed within 10 business days
- Reimbursements deposited via direct deposit

8.4 Currency Conversion
- International expenses converted at the exchange rate on the date of purchase
- Use the rate shown on your credit card statement
- The company does not reimburse for unfavorable exchange rate differences

---

SECTION 9: COMPLIANCE

9.1 Audit
- Expense reports are subject to random audits by the Finance team
- Managers must review and approve all direct reports' expenses
- Flagged expenses require additional documentation within 5 business days

9.2 Violations
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Denial of reimbursement
- Requirement to repay improperly claimed expenses
- Revocation of corporate credit card
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- For repeated or intentional violations: potential legal action

9.3 Exceptions
Exceptions to any provision of this policy require written approval from the CFO. Exception requests should be submitted through the Finance portal with business justification.

---

SECTION 10: SUSTAINABILITY

10.1 Green Travel Initiatives
ACME encourages environmentally conscious travel:
- Choose video conferencing when possible over in-person meetings
- Use public transportation when practical
- Choose direct flights (lower carbon footprint)
- Stay at hotels with sustainability certifications
- Opt for electric/hybrid rental vehicles when available

10.2 Carbon Offset
ACME automatically purchases carbon offsets for all business air travel. This cost is borne by the company and does not count against departmental travel budgets.
