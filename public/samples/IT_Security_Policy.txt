ACME CORPORATION - IT SECURITY POLICY
=======================================
Version 3.2 | Effective: February 1, 2025
Classification: Internal Use Only

---

SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes the information security requirements for all ACME Corporation employees, contractors, and third-party users who access company systems, data, or networks.

1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and consultants with system access
- Third-party vendors with network connectivity
- All company-owned and personal devices used for work

---

SECTION 2: ACCEPTABLE USE

2.1 General Use
Company systems and networks are provided primarily for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties, violate any policies, or create security risks.

2.2 Prohibited Activities
The following activities are strictly prohibited:
- Accessing or distributing illegal content
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Installing unauthorized software on company devices
- Sharing credentials or passwords with others
- Circumventing security controls
- Using company resources for personal commercial ventures
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the corporate network
- Downloading or using pirated software

2.3 Email Usage
- Company email is for business communications
- Do not open attachments from unknown senders
- Report phishing attempts to security@acme.com immediately
- Do not auto-forward company email to personal accounts
- Email attachments over 25 MB should be shared via approved file sharing platforms

---

SECTION 3: PASSWORD POLICY

3.1 Password Requirements
All passwords must meet the following criteria:
- Minimum 14 characters in length
- Must contain at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character
- Cannot contain your name, username, or common dictionary words
- Cannot reuse any of your last 12 passwords

3.2 Password Management
- Passwords must be changed every 90 days
- Use the company-approved password manager (1Password) for storing credentials
- Never write passwords on paper or store them in plain text files
- Never share passwords via email, chat, or phone

3.3 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
MFA is mandatory for:
- All VPN connections
- Email access from new devices
- Cloud service logins (AWS, Azure, GCP)
- HR and financial systems
- Code repositories and CI/CD pipelines

---

SECTION 4: DATA CLASSIFICATION

4.1 Classification Levels
All company data must be classified into one of four levels:

PUBLIC: Information approved for external distribution
- Marketing materials, press releases, public website content

INTERNAL: Information for internal use only
- Internal memos, meeting notes, project documentation

CONFIDENTIAL: Sensitive business information
- Financial reports, strategic plans, employee records, customer data

RESTRICTED: Highest sensitivity
- Trade secrets, encryption keys, security audit reports, PII/PHI data

4.2 Handling Requirements by Classification

PUBLIC:
- No special handling required
- Can be shared externally

INTERNAL:
- Share only with ACME employees
- Store on company systems only
- No special encryption required

CONFIDENTIAL:
- Share only on need-to-know basis
- Must be encrypted in transit and at rest
- Access must be logged and audited
- Must not be stored on personal devices

RESTRICTED:
- Requires explicit authorization for access
- Must be encrypted with AES-256 or equivalent
- Access logged and reviewed monthly
- Cannot be copied or transmitted without CISO approval
- Must be permanently deleted when no longer needed

---

SECTION 5: DEVICE SECURITY

5.1 Company Devices
- Full disk encryption must be enabled (BitLocker/FileVault)
- Automatic screen lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
- Antivirus software must be installed and kept updated
- Operating system updates must be applied within 72 hours of release
- Critical security patches must be applied within 24 hours

5.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)
Employees using personal devices for work must:
- Enroll the device in the company MDM (Mobile Device Management) system
- Enable device encryption
- Set a device PIN/password of at least 6 digits
- Allow remote wipe capability for company data
- Keep the operating system updated
- Not use jailbroken or rooted devices

5.3 Lost or Stolen Devices
Report any lost or stolen device to IT Security immediately:
- Phone: (415) 555-HELP (4357)
- Email: security@acme.com
- Slack: #it-security-urgent
IT will remotely wipe the device within 1 hour of report.

---

SECTION 6: NETWORK SECURITY

6.1 Corporate Network
- The corporate network is segmented into zones: Production, Development, Corporate, Guest
- Access between zones requires explicit firewall rules
- All network traffic is monitored and logged
- Unauthorized network scanning or penetration testing is prohibited

6.2 Wi-Fi Policy
- Corporate Wi-Fi requires certificate-based authentication
- Guest Wi-Fi is isolated from corporate network
- Do not connect personal IoT devices to corporate Wi-Fi
- Report any suspicious or unknown access points

6.3 VPN Requirements
- VPN must be used for all remote access to corporate resources
- Split tunneling is disabled by default
- VPN sessions timeout after 12 hours and require re-authentication
- Only the company-approved VPN client (Cisco AnyConnect) may be used

---

SECTION 7: REMOTE ACCESS SECURITY

7.1 Remote Work Security
Remote workers must comply with the following:
- Use company-approved VPN for all work activities
- Ensure home Wi-Fi uses WPA3 encryption (minimum WPA2)
- Work in a private area where screens cannot be observed by others
- Do not use public Wi-Fi for work without VPN
- Lock screens when stepping away, even at home
- Ensure home network router firmware is updated

7.2 Public Locations
When working from public locations (cafes, airports, etc.):
- Always use VPN
- Use a privacy screen filter
- Do not leave devices unattended
- Do not discuss confidential information in public
- Disable Bluetooth and AirDrop

7.3 Travel Security
When traveling for business:
- Enable device tracking (Find My Device)
- Carry devices in carry-on luggage only
- Use hotel safe for devices when not in use
- Avoid using hotel business center computers for company work
- Be cautious of social engineering at conferences

---

SECTION 8: SOFTWARE AND APPLICATION SECURITY

8.1 Approved Software
Only software from the approved software catalog may be installed on company devices. The catalog is maintained on the IT portal. Requests for new software must be submitted through IT Service Desk.

8.2 Software Updates
- Enable automatic updates where possible
- Apply security patches within the timelines specified in Section 5.1
- Report any software that cannot be updated to IT Security

8.3 Cloud Services
- Only approved cloud services may be used for company data
- Approved services: Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub, AWS, Jira, Confluence, Figma, Notion
- Unapproved services (Shadow IT) must not be used for company data
- Requests for new cloud services go through the IT procurement process

---

SECTION 9: INCIDENT RESPONSE

9.1 Reporting Security Incidents
All employees must immediately report:
- Suspected phishing emails
- Unauthorized access attempts
- Malware or virus infections
- Lost or stolen devices
- Suspicious behavior or social engineering attempts
- Data breaches or potential data exposure

9.2 How to Report
- Email: security@acme.com
- Slack: #it-security-urgent
- Phone: (415) 555-HELP (4357)
- In person: Visit IT Security on Floor 3

9.3 Incident Response Process
1. Detection: Incident is identified and reported
2. Triage: Security team assesses severity and impact
3. Containment: Immediate actions to limit damage
4. Investigation: Root cause analysis
5. Remediation: Fix vulnerabilities and restore systems
6. Recovery: Return to normal operations
7. Post-Incident Review: Lessons learned and process improvements

---

SECTION 10: COMPLIANCE AND TRAINING

10.1 Security Training
- All employees must complete annual security awareness training
- New employees must complete training within first 2 weeks
- Additional role-specific training for IT staff, developers, and data handlers
- Quarterly phishing simulation exercises

10.2 Compliance
This policy aligns with:
- SOC 2 Type II
- ISO 27001
- GDPR (for EU operations)
- CCPA (for California customer data)
- HIPAA (for healthcare-related projects)

10.3 Policy Violations
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Revocation of system access
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Legal action where applicable
- Reporting to relevant authorities for criminal activity

---

SECTION 11: DATA BACKUP AND RECOVERY

11.1 Backup Policy
- All company data is backed up daily to encrypted off-site storage
- Database backups run every 6 hours
- Backup retention: 30 days for daily backups, 12 months for monthly backups
- Backups are tested quarterly through restoration exercises

11.2 Disaster Recovery
- Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 4 hours for critical systems
- Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 1 hour for critical data
- Annual disaster recovery drills are conducted
- DR plan is reviewed and updated semi-annually
